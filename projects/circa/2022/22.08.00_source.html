US House Speaker Nancy Pelosi's visit to Taiwan triggered China's largest-ever military exercises around the island. The Zaporizhzhia Nuclear Power Plant became a major international crisis as Russian rockets damaged the facility. Both Russia and Ukraine blamed each other for shelling attacks that hit the plant multiple times, with international atomic energy inspectors warning of a potential nuclear catastrophe.

Pakistan declared a "climate catastrophe" as devastating floods killed more than 1,000 people and caused an estimated \$30 billion in damage. The flooding was the world's deadliest since 2017, with houses, crops, and key infrastructure swept away across the country.

The FBI executed an unprecedented search warrant at Mar-a-Lago, former President Trump's Florida residence, as part of a Justice Department investigation into classified documents. The search marked the first time in US history that federal agents had searched a former president's home, triggering intense political controversy.

Mikhail Gorbachev, the final leader of the Soviet Union, died at age 91. His death marked the end of an era, as Gorbachev had presided over the Soviet Union's peaceful dissolution and helped end the Cold War through his policies of glasnost and perestroika.
