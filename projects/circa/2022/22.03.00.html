Source Signal: March 2022

Russia-Ukraine War Escalates to Full-Scale Conflict: Russian forces intensified attacks on populated urban areas, with explosions in Kharkiv and an attack on Kyiv's TV tower. The Siege of Mariupol became one of the war's most devastating battles. The Zaporizhzhia Nuclear Power Plant caught fire after Russian shelling, creating global fears of a nuclear catastrophe.

Unprecedented Global Sanctions Target Russia: The international response involved a massive sanctions regime. Canada and the UK imposed new restrictions, while major corporations like Samsung, PayPal, Visa, and Mastercard suspended services in Russia. JPMorgan Chase warned the crisis could exceed the 1998 Russian financial collapse.

Humanitarian Crisis and Refugee Exodus: The war triggered Europe's largest refugee crisis since 1945, with millions of Ukrainians fleeing. UN access to besieged Mariupol remained blocked despite thousands of civilian deaths.

North Korea's Military Escalation: North Korea conducted its first full intercontinental ballistic missile test since 2017, launching the Hwasong-17. Analysts determined the missile could put the entire mainland United States within striking distance.
