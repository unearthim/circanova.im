Source Signal: November 2020

Biden Wins US Election Amid Trump's Refusal to Concede: Joe Biden was declared the winner of the US presidential election, but President Trump refused to concede, launching lawsuits and claiming widespread voter fraud, creating a period of profound political uncertainty.

COVID-19 Vaccine Breakthroughs Offer Hope: Moderna and Pfizer both announced their vaccines were highly effective, marking a major turning point and providing the first real hope for an end to the pandemic.

US Experiences Worst COVID Surge Yet: Simultaneously, the US surpassed 11 million cases and 250,000 deaths, experiencing its most intense and deadly wave of the virus to date.

"Stop the Steal" Protests Erupt: Protests supporting President Trump's claims of a stolen election occurred in cities across the US, sometimes leading to clashes and arrests.

Vienna Terror Attack: A deadly mass shooting in Vienna, claimed by the Islamic State, shocked Europe and highlighted ongoing security threats.

Commentary: The Contested Reality

November 2020 was a month defined by paradox. An election was both over and not over. A cure was found while the plague was at its worst. This created two parallel, mutually exclusive realities that the world was forced to process simultaneously.

The glitch of this era is this "contested reality." It's the catastrophic failure of a system trying to display contradictory data as a single, coherent truth.

This artifact simulates an electoral results screen breaking down under this paradoxical strain. It flickers violently between the declared outcome and the contested narrative, unable to resolve the data. In the background, a new signal of hope—the vaccine—tries to pierce the oppressive static of the pandemic's worst surge. The ghost in this machine is certainty itself, shattered into a million flickering, unreliable pieces.
